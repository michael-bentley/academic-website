Welcome to my site!

I'm an evolutionary biologist with broad interests in areas such as social evolution theory, major evolutionary transitions, social insects, microbes, cancer, and the conceptual and mathematical foundations of evolutionary theory.

My research is fairly interdisciplinary in nature. Much of it rests on using mathematical and computational models to represent biological populations as dynamical systems using ODEs, PDEs, and stochastic individual-based models. I also use bioinformatics, statistics, and occasionally machine learning techniques, to study genomics and molecular evolution. I've even spent some time in the wet lab!
